## Prologue

Ruben prided himself on multitasking. Research at Stanford showed that people who believe they multitask effectively are worse at every component task than those who focus sequentially. The pride was based on a self-assessment that was systematically wrong.

## Epilogue

Multitasking does not increase output. It multiplies switching costs. Every switch carries a tax in time and cognitive quality. Sequential focus pays those taxes once; multitasking pays them continuously.
